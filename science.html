3rd Grade TEKS — Scientific Practices, Matter, Forces, Earth & Organisms

🔭 Scientific Practices ▼
  • Plan and conduct investigations; ask questions and make observations
  • Collect, measure, and record data using metric units
  • Use tools: thermometers, rulers, graduated cylinders, hand lenses, balances, spring scales
  • Organize data into tables, charts, and bar graphs
  • Analyze data and draw reasonable conclusions from evidence
  • Understand that repeated tests increase reliability
  • Know the limitations of models (size, materials, properties)
  • Practice lab safety: safety goggles, gloves, proper disposal
  • Evaluate product claims using evidence and reasoning

⚗️ Matter and Energy ▼
  • Describe and classify matter as solid, liquid, or gas
  • Understand that solids have definite shape; liquids and gases take the shape of their container
  • Measure physical properties: temperature, mass, magnetism, sink/float
  • Predict changes in state caused by heating or cooling (melting, freezing, evaporation, condensation)
  • Explore mixtures — two or more materials combined (sand + gravel, metal + plastic clips)

⚡ Force, Motion, and Energy ▼
  • Identify forms of energy: mechanical, light, sound, heat/thermal
  • Understand that pushing and pulling changes position and motion (swings, balls, wagons)
  • Observe how gravity pulls objects toward Earth
  • Understand magnetism — like poles repel, opposite poles attract
  • Greater force = greater change in motion

🌍 Earth and Space ▼
  • Understand how soil forms — weathering of rock + decomposition of organisms
  • Identify rapid Earth changes: volcanoes, earthquakes, landslides
  • Identify landforms: mountains, hills, valleys, plains
  • Measure and record weather: temperature, wind direction, precipitation
  • Understand the Sun as a star that provides light and heat for the water cycle
  • Know the orbits of Earth and Moon (Earth orbits Sun, Moon orbits Earth)
  • Name the 8 planets in order from the Sun: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune
  • Identify and conserve natural resources

🌿 Organisms and Environment ▼
  • Describe environments and the populations that live in them (ecosystems)
  • Identify the flow of energy in a food chain (producer → consumer → decomposer)
  • Predict how changes in a food chain affect the ecosystem
  • Describe how organisms respond to environmental changes (floods, droughts)
  • Identify structures and adaptations that help animals and plants survive
  • Distinguish inherited traits from learned behaviors
  • Describe life cycles: tomato plant, frog, butterfly, ladybug, chicken
