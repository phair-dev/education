Social Studies

3rd Grade TEKS — History, Geography, Economics, Government & More

🏛️ History ▼
  • Identify key historical figures: Pierre-Charles L'Enfant, Benjamin Banneker, Benjamin Franklin
  • Know explorers and founders: Daniel Boone, Christopher Columbus, Juan de Oñate, Founding Fathers
  • Describe why people form communities (security, religion, education, well-being)
  • Use chronology vocabulary: past, present, future; year, decade (10 years), century (100 years)
  • Read and create timelines; sequence historical events

🗺️ Geography ▼
  • Identify physical features: climate, landforms (mountains, plains, valleys, deltas), natural resources, natural hazards
  • Explain how people adapt to or modify their environment (deserts, mountains, wetlands, plains)
  • Describe effects of physical processes: volcanoes, hurricanes, earthquakes
  • Describe effects of human processes: building homes, conservation, pollution
  • Use cardinal (N/S/E/W) and intermediate (NE/SW, etc.) directions
  • Use a map scale to calculate distance
  • Read map elements: title, compass rose, legend, scale, grid system

💵 Economics ▼
  • Identify ways of earning, spending, saving, and donating money
  • Create a simple budget
  • Define scarcity — not enough resources to meet all wants and needs
  • Explain supply and demand — when supply drops, prices rise
  • Explain the U.S. free enterprise system
  • Understand how production costs affect profit
  • Identify business entrepreneurs: Henry Ford, Sam Walton, Milton Hershey, Mary Kay Ash

🏛️ Government ▼
  • Describe local, state, and national government structure
  • Identify how government officials are chosen (elections)
  • Identify government services: schools, police, fire, roads
  • Explain how government services are financed (taxes)
  • Know the purpose of the Declaration of Independence and U.S. Constitution
  • Understand the Bill of Rights — protects citizens' basic freedoms
  • Explain "consent of the governed" — government gets its power from the people

🗳️ Citizenship ▼
  • Identify characteristics of good citizenship: truthfulness, justice, equality, respect, responsibility
  • Know civic heroes: Helen Keller, Clara Barton, Ruby Bridges, military & first responders
  • Identify civic responsibilities: obeying laws, serving the community, voting, jury duty
  • Explain how individual or group decisions can improve a community
  • Understand nonprofit organizations like the Red Cross and how they help

🎭 Culture ▼
  • Explain the significance of cultural celebrations in different communities
  • Know state and national heroes: Hector P. Garcia, James A. Lovell, Ellen Ochoa, Todd Beamer
  • Know historical heroes: Harriet Tubman, Juliette Gordon Low
  • Identify writers and artists: Laura Ingalls Wilder, Kadir Nelson, Phillis Wheatley, Carmen Lomas Garza

🚀 Science, Technology, and Society ▼
  • Identify scientists and inventors: Jonas Salk (polio vaccine), Maria Mitchell (comet), Louis Pasteur (pasteurization), Bill Gates (computers)
  • Explain the impact of medical vaccines — allowed people to live longer, healthier lives
  • Explain how pasteurization made food safer
  • Understand how computers have transformed communities

📋 Social Studies Skills ▼
  • Research information using print, oral, visual, and internet resources
  • Sequence and categorize information
  • Identify main idea, fact vs. opinion, cause and effect, compare and contrast
  • Use book parts: table of contents, glossary, index
  • Read and interpret graphs, charts, tables, timelines, and maps
  • Distinguish primary sources (firsthand accounts) from secondary sources
  • Apply problem-solving and decision-making processes
